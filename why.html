Why | 2025 | het Platencabinet

Why

I forgot who I was and became ‘yours’
Now I look back, and all that’s left are open sores
You never cared—you might as well never have
Because our history is wrapped in distress
My heart is no longer broken, but still a mess


And I know I am not the one to blame
You drained all positives, tried to fill me up with shame
Then left me broken—now I can never be the same


You took my light, but my heart still shines the brightest
Now you’re gone, I can dance—I feel the lightness
And even though healing takes its time
I am rested, I am whole—knowing it’s all mine


Now I lay alone in bed, sometimes happy, sometimes sad
My body lost in the memories of warmer nights
Skipping and forgetting all the fights
But the lonely memory remains—it still feels the same


They say time heals, that if you work on yourself, it will be over before you know it
I guess I am impatient because I need to know when I can move on—please
When will I feel secure in love and let go when my body says, ‘enough of this’?
Will I ever be able to?
Or did you steal that away too?


Looking at the night sky and feeling the cold breeze
Reminds me to take a break and breathe with ease
And as long as I have the night sky
I know I will survive
